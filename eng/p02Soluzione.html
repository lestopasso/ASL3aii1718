The Water We Drink

So much effort to bring drinking water to your taps at home, and then you prefer to drink bottled water! Don’t you trust it? Despite the fact that our tap water is checked daily in analytical laboratories? It is a legal requirement: the work of the laboratories starts at the source and follows the course of the water, frequent samples being taken at various points for analysis to ensure that it arrives pure and safe to drink. It is also a "farm-to-table" product: it does not travel in a lorry on the roads (like most bottled water), but reaches your home via the aqueduct network, without doing harm to the environment. And it travels light, with no packaging.
Every year, more than twelve billion litres of water are bottled in Italy, 80% of which is in PET (polyethylene terephthalate) bottles, which could cover a surface equal to 4,300 football pitches! Furthermore, aqueduct water costs much less, up to one thousand times less,not to mention the effort… Try walking upstairs with two crates of bottled water! And if tap water has a slight taste of chlorine, just fill up a jug and put it in the fridge: the chlorine evaporates and you won’t even taste the difference.
