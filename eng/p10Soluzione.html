Energising Waste

And if you don’t recycle? Not all types of waste can go into the recycling chain. Some cannot be recycled, others end up in the wrong container when they are sorted and can no longer be used. However, instead of these ending up in landfill sites, some types of waste can be transformed into energy.
The waste-to-energy plants differ from the old incinerators as they don’t just burn waste, but produce electricity from the waste matter itself and, in some cases,
even produce heat which is then transported to homes by a district heating network. In addition, the emissions of waste-to-energy plants - thanks to technological evolution, which has allowed us to minimize the environmental impact - are sometimes lower, for certain parameters, than those resulting from the fossil fuels that are used to produce energy.
