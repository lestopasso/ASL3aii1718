Low-consumption Citizens

A smart city is inhabited by citizens who use resources in a mindful and sustainable way. Smart citizens produce, use and exchange clean energy with the grid and know their consumption in real time. They live in smart houses, constructed with green building techniques, controlled by domotics which monitors all the home electrical appliances.
The objects in the home communicates with each other, with the city and with the citizen in an “Internet of Things” network. These innovations are spreading, and over the next few decades, they will become commonplace. But you can already change your habits and adopt responsible behaviour. May we give you some advice?

  • Incandescent light bulbs have been banned, replace any existing ones in your home with low-consumption light bulbs, possibly LED;

  • Don’t leave televisions, computers or printers on stand-by;

  • Don’t put hot food in the fridge;

  • Switch off the light when you leave a room;

  • Choose the energy class of electrical appliances carefully, remembering that the higher the class, the lower the consumption;

  • Use the washing machine and the dishwasher only with a full load;

  • If it is warm, don’t open doors and windows. Instead, turn down the temperature of the radiators;

  • Change old window and door frames to insulate your home against the heat and cold;

  • Set the temperature of the air conditioning in a way that avoids sharp differences between indoor and outdoor temperatures.

There is a lot you can do to become sustainable and the contribution of each and every citizen is essential. We hope this short journey into the world of sustainability has been useful to you and that you will become more mindful and more ecological in the future.
